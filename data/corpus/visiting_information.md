# Visiting the Verhoeven Museum

The Verhoeven Museum of Northern Light is located in the fictional town of Aalderen in the northern Netherlands. This document describes general visiting arrangements for the museum.

The museum is open Tuesday to Sunday and is closed on Mondays. It is also closed on 25 December and 1 January. The permanent collection is displayed across the original 1911 building, while temporary exhibitions are shown in the Verhoeven Annexe.

Admission includes access to both the permanent collection and any current temporary exhibition. The museum offers a reduced rate for students and free entry for children under twelve. Guided tours of the permanent collection are available on weekend afternoons.

The museum has a small reference library, available to researchers by appointment, holding material on the Aalderen School and the history of the Verhoeven Foundation. The library does not lend material and its holdings must be consulted on site.

Photography without flash is permitted in the permanent collection but not in temporary exhibitions. The museum café is located in the Verhoeven Annexe and overlooks the sculpture courtyard.
