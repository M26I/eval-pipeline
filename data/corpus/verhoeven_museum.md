# The Verhoeven Museum of Northern Light

The Verhoeven Museum of Northern Light is a fictional institution located in the invented town of Aalderen, in the northern Netherlands. It was founded in 1911 by the industrialist Cornelis Verhoeven to house his personal collection of regional landscape painting. The museum opened to the public on 3 September 1911.

The museum specialises in the so-called Aalderen School, a fictional movement of landscape and interior painters active between roughly 1840 and 1920, known for their treatment of low, diffuse northern daylight.

The permanent collection holds 412 works across painting, drawing, and printmaking. The museum's best-known holdings are the interior scenes of Margriet Dekker and the coastal studies of Pieter van Loon.

The building itself was designed by the architect Hendrik Bos in a restrained neoclassical style. A modern wing, the Verhoeven Annexe, was added in 1978 to house temporary exhibitions and the museum's conservation studio.

The museum is governed by the Verhoeven Foundation, established under the terms of Cornelis Verhoeven's will in 1909. The current director is Dr. Saskia Meijer, appointed in 2019.
