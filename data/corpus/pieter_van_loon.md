# Pieter van Loon (1828–1901)

Pieter van Loon was a fictional marine and coastal painter, regarded as one of the founders of the Aalderen School. He was born in the port town of Zeewijk on 9 July 1828 and died in Aalderen on 21 January 1901.

Van Loon is known for his coastal studies of the northern shoreline, particularly his depictions of flat grey seas under overcast skies. His technique emphasised thin, layered glazes to capture the quality of diffuse maritime light.

His best-known painting, "Grey Sea, Low Tide" (1867), hangs in the main gallery of the Verhoeven Museum. The work is often cited as the defining image of the Aalderen School's approach to atmosphere.

Van Loon taught several younger painters, the most notable being Margriet Dekker, who studied under him in the early 1870s before turning to interior subjects. He was known as a demanding but generous teacher.

In later life van Loon suffered from failing eyesight, and his output after 1890 declined sharply. The Verhoeven Museum holds fourteen of his paintings and a large group of preparatory sketches.
