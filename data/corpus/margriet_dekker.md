# Margriet Dekker (1851–1919)

Margriet Dekker was a fictional painter associated with the Aalderen School, best known for her domestic interior scenes. She was born in Aalderen on 14 March 1851 and died there on 2 November 1919.

Dekker trained under the marine painter Pieter van Loon before developing her own focus on interior subjects. She is celebrated for her handling of window light falling across tiled floors and plain plastered walls.

Her most famous work, "Woman Reading by a North Window" (1883), is considered the centrepiece of the Verhoeven Museum's collection. The painting depicts a solitary figure seated beside a leaded window, and is noted for its restrained palette of greys, ochres, and muted blues.

Other significant works include "The Blue Kitchen" (1888) and "Morning Correspondence" (1895), both held by the Verhoeven Museum. Dekker rarely dated her drawings, which has complicated the chronology of her graphic work.

Dekker never married and lived for most of her life in a house on the Aalderen canal known as the Witte Huis. She bequeathed the contents of her studio to the Verhoeven Foundation in 1919.
