# Woman Reading by a North Window (1883)

"Woman Reading by a North Window" is a fictional oil painting by Margriet Dekker, completed in 1883. It is the most famous work in the collection of the Verhoeven Museum of Northern Light, where it has hung since the museum's opening in 1911.

The painting measures 78 by 61 centimetres and is executed in oil on canvas. It depicts a single female figure seated in a plain interior, reading beside a leaded window through which soft northern light enters from the left.

The palette is restrained, dominated by greys, ochres, and muted blues. Critics have long admired the way Dekker renders the fall of light across the tiled floor and the plastered wall behind the figure.

The identity of the sitter is unknown. It has been suggested that the figure is Dekker's younger sister, but there is no documentary evidence for this, and the museum describes the sitter only as unidentified.

The painting was acquired directly from the artist by Cornelis Verhoeven in 1901 and formed part of the founding bequest of the museum. It has never left the collection and has been loaned externally only once, for a fictional 1962 exhibition in Amsterdam.
